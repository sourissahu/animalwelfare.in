VISION

Our vision is to create a world where every street dog is safe, healthy, loved, and respected — living free from cruelty, hunger, and neglect.

MOTIVE

We are driven by a deep compassion for voiceless animals and a commitment to make a difference — not just for ourselves, but for the countless street dogs in need of care, protection, and dignity. Our mission is to serve, heal, and uplift their lives, making our society kinder and more humane.

MISSION

Our mission is to rescue and rehabilitate street dogs by providing food, medical care, shelter, and a safe environment. We are committed to controlling the street dog population through sterilization and vaccination drives, while promoting adoption and spreading awareness to build a compassionate and responsible community.

WHY WE DO WHAT WE DO?

Everyone aspires to be the epitome of something meaningful — and we strive to be the epitome of HAPPYPAWS — a real, compassionate change that makes our campus a safer, kinder, and more sustainable place for both humans and animals.

At HAPPYPAWS, we believe every life has a purpose, and street dogs are no exception. Our mission is to care for them with love, responsibility, and respect — by ensuring timely feeding, medical treatment, vaccinations, and sterilization. We aim to create a peaceful coexistence, where dog lovers can nurture these lives while those with fears can feel secure in a well-managed, balanced environment.

This journey is not for the faint-hearted. It demands commitment, compassion, and a sense of duty — not just towards animals, but towards our community as a whole. HAPPYPAWS is more than a name — it is a reflection of every individual who chooses to step up, speak for the voiceless, and work for a future where harmony is the norm.

PROJECTS

When we started, our primary concern was simply ensuring that the street dogs on campus didn’t go hungry. What began as the “Feed the Paw-Friends” Drive has since grown into a dedicated initiative. Over time, we’ve evolved as a group, taking up various projects and events focused on the welfare of street dogs and fostering a harmonious environment on campus. With continued passion and teamwork, we remain committed to this cause.

Feeding Drive

We began by collecting surplus food from the campus mess and feeding the often-overlooked pups around quieter corners of the campus. What started as a small gesture of care gradually turned into a thoughtful initiative. During winters, we make efforts to arrange warm shelter spaces for street dogs, ensuring they are safe and protected from the cold. We also keep a close eye on expecting mother dogs, offering them special care and attention during such vulnerable times. We believe that even if our actions don’t spark immediate change, they sow the seeds of compassion and responsibility — inspiring others to notice, care, and contribute. Every small step matters when it comes to creating a more humane and inclusive environment.

Treatment Drive

Another important initiative we've taken up is our “Heal the Paws” Drive, focused on the medical care of street dogs. From treating sick and injured pups to providing special care for mother dogs, our efforts aim to ensure every dog gets the attention they deserve. We regularly coordinate treatments for skin diseases, infections, and injuries, and also work on sterilization (neutering/spaying) to help manage the stray dog population in a humane and effective way. Through timely care and intervention, we strive to reduce suffering and promote a healthier life for these voiceless beings — one treatment at a time.
